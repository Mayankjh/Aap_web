Public Policy Research (PPR)

Aam Aadmi Party – Public Policy Research (PPR)

The focus of the Public Policy Research fellowship​ is to build an analytical, economic and political understanding of the policy-making process, policy-implementation process and the functioning of the government structure in the Indian context, specifically in the context of Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi (GNCTD).

The curriculum dwells into various political ideologies that drive the Public Policymaking process. Throughout the course, the selected fellows will understand and research based on various ideological policy methods such as Libertarian Public Policy, Socialist Public Policy, Federal Policy and Democratic Policy.

The fellowship will also concentrate on texts and research papers that have been specifically selected or formulated for the very purpose of broadening the understanding of systems, structures, economics, and politics. There will be a modest amount of focus on the intersection of Data Analytics and Public Policy.

Curriculum

The Week wise curriculum will be as follows

Week 1

  • Introduction to Stakeholder’s Analysis: 3 Stakeholders to Governance in Delhi
  • The Multiplicity of Authority (MoA) and its implications: Case Study.
  • Statehood for Delhi: Critical Analysis of Governance Models of Capital territories in the developed world.
  • Task 1 Submission and Review.

Reading​:

  • Prisoner’s of Geography​ by Tim Marshall
  • The Republic​ by Plato

Week 2

  • Introduction to Libertarian Public Policy Making: Political ideologies that drive policy-making
  • Analysis of Delhi Government’s Education Policy
  • Analysis of Delhi Government’s Health Policy
  • Task 2& 3 Submission and Review

Reading​:

  • India After Gandhi​ by Ramchandra Guha

Week 3

  • Right to Food, Clothing, Housing: A Utopian future or a genuine a possibility?
  • Universal Basic Income (UBI) vs Unemployment Allowance (UA)
  • Scandinavia: The Ideal Socialist St
  • Task 4 & 5 Submission and Review

Reading​:

  • Bourgeois Equality: How Ideas, Not Capital or Institutions, Enriched the World​ by Deirdre N. McCloskey
  • The Rational Optimist: How Prosperity Evolves​ by Matt Ridley

Week 4

  • The Intersection of Data Analytics and Public Policy
  • India’s Railway Policy: Socialist origins and existing shortcomings
  • Indian Ports and Trade: Geopolitical and economic relevance
  • Task 6 & 7 Submission and Review

Reading​:

  • On Tyranny: Twenty Lessons from the Twentieth Century ​by Timothy D. Snyder
  • Real Heroes: Inspiring True Stories of Courage, Character, and Conviction ​by Lawrence W. Reed

Selection Process:

  • The candidates will be selected on the basis of their aptitude to critically understand policy, economics, and politics.
  • The candidates would also be shortlisted on the basis of their availability of 3
  • The application process is followed by an interview of shortlisted candidates.
  • The interview will be either in person or telephonic.
  • Shortlisted candidates will be asked to furnish references.
  • Note: Any attempt to influence the selection process could lead to the disqualification of the candidate

Fellowship Program Particulars:

  • The Fellowship will commence from 13th August onwards
  • All students and young professional under the age of 30 years are eligible
  • Deadline for applications is 5th August 2018, 11:59 pm and selected fellows will be notified by 10th August.
  • The Course will be held in Delhi. Fellows must make their own arrangements for stay and travel
