Internship Programs

Public Policy Research

During the course of the internship, the interns would be taught how to use public policy as the principled guide to action taken by the administrative executive branches with regard to a class of issues, in a manner consistent with law and institutional customs. They’d be dealing with polity, governance, law, economics, sociology and policy making. A variety of literature, videos, reading exercises, debate sessions and research papers would be used to train the interns. The department would make the interns adept at conducting professional research, integrating developments in the areas of education, livelihood, governance, urban reforms and environment.

Social Media Marketing

The programme deals with understanding the process of gaining traffic or attention through social media sites. Understanding marketing on Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, WhatsApp - and learning about all the facets of internet marketing. The interns will be trained to use social media to help build links, spread and market different contents on both official and unofficial platforms. They’d be working with special focus on identifying target audiences and creating channels to reach out to them thereby achieving marketing and branding goals. The programme would also teach the interns how to formulate social media marketing and online campaigning strategies.

Apply

Graphic Design

The graphic design team aims at designing visual compositions that would be able to effectively communicate ideas to larger audiences. During the programme, the interns would be entrusted with producing graphic content around political, economic and social issues. They would be required to be in loop with the current topics and generate suitable content. The programme includes developing information graphics on public policy and government decisions. The interns would be trained in using typography, visual arts, page layout techniques, colour palettes, font types and sizes, content placement, etc. to create visual compositions that would capture the target audience’s attention and expand the reach of the party.

Apply

Field Research

The programme revolves around surveying large sample sizes at the ground level throughout Delhi. It aims at collecting primary data, using methods such as personal and telephonic interviews, and direct observation. The data collected would be used for policy research and policy making. The interns would be required to conduct field surveys and find about difficulties and issues faced by the people at the ground level. The researchers would also be compiling primary data for electoral purposes. They would also be engaging in public discussions, group activities, analysis of the documents produced within the group- all of which would add qualitative dimensions to an essentially quantitative work.

Apply

Data Analytics

During the course of the internship, data analysts would be processing data sets in order to draw conclusions about the information they contain, with the aid of specialized systems and software. The interns working on and analysing various data sets and refining them on Python. They’d be made adept at using data analytics technologies and techniques which are widely used in commercial industries to enable organizations to make more-informed business decisions. It would also include gaining a comprehensive understanding of Python and building machine learning algorithms for policy making and for electoral usage. The interns would be trained to use data analytics as a tool to increase revenues, improve operational efficiency, optimize marketing campaigns and respond more quickly to emerging market trends and gain a competitive edge over rivals.

Virtual Internships

This programme is especially designed for those who are based outside Delhi or are not available on a full time basis for the internship. The internship curriculum varies from department to department and the applicants can choose any one that interests them the most. The internship would offer flexible timings and other such incentives. The virtual internships would be handled via Google drives and WhatsApp groups.

Media and Public Relations

The department deals with how our organisation deals with the media and the public. The interns would have to carry out media researches on political and social issues pertinent to the contemporary times and coordinate with the media houses to further the party’s agenda. The programme seeks to train the interns in the field of media management and looking after public relation activities for political campaigns. The department would also deal with formulating press releases and press responses in both Hindi and English. We also wish to expand our base and reach out to more colleges and other institutions.

Content Writing

The Content Writing internship concerns with generating content for specific target audiences. The programme would deal with two different areas in content writing; one for article and blog writing and the other generating content for social media and web based platforms. We aim at training the writers to be able to produce relevant, quality content. The Department aims at producing engaging content for both online and offline audiences and generating tightly focussed content for blogs, articles, tweets, videos, social media posts, etc. that are written with great clarity and are full of persuasive power.

Apply

Legal Research

The programme revolves around building research papers on topical and current legal issues, and engaging interns in comprehensive research on legal rights and the intricacies of the legal system and research work. It would include finding answers to legal question or checking for legal precedent that can be cited in a brief or at trials being fought by lawyers representing the party. The programme would also train the interns in the process of filing RTIs to draw information from various government organisations as well as in the technique of formulating research strategies and advancing one’s potential of analysing legal problems.

Apply

Human Resource Management

The Human Resource Management (HRM) programme would revolve around the formal systems devised for the management of people within an organization, primarily dealing with the responsibilities of staffing, employee compensation and benefits, and defining/designing work. The purpose of HRM is to maximize the productivity of an organization by optimizing the effectiveness of its employees. The interns would be made to understand the workings of political organisations and how they’re different in terms of their organizing and management systems. They’d also undergo rigorous training and would be building and running systems and engaging in managerial operations. The aim of the interns would be to establish effective and efficient channels of communication and using technology to manage tasks. The programme also includes a literature based curriculum, making the interns well versed in both theoretical and practical management.

Videography and Photography

The videography and photography team wold be working producing video and photo content at the ground level and capture social and political issues that we be used in running political campaigns. Teams would also be working on producing video films for various social media platforms, keeping in mind the target audiences and generating simple, yet hard hitting content that would connect and engage with the audiences. The interns would also be required to make documentaries for political for the AAP government that would highlight its achievements in the field of education, healthcare, etc

Leadership and Skill Development

We aim at building confident speakers and debaters out of our interns through a series of intense sessions on public speaking, managerial operations and skill enhancement. Through this programme we wish to equip our interns with skill sets that would help them in their career development. Sessions on understanding texts and analysing them, reading, campaign development, etc. would help instil leadership skills the ability to self-manage, solve problems and understand the political environment. Developing the skills to adapt to different roles, working within a team, inculcating people management skills- would all help the potential to lead by influence.
