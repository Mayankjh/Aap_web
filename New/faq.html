Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be an AAP supporter to be a part of this fellowship programme?
Certainly not! Our fellowships cover various topics, from public policy, to data analytics; from HR management to legal research. We would be doing readings, case studies and real world problem solving. As a party, we consist of individuals of many schools of thought. However, we all have one thing in common though: we dream of an India of equal opportunity across all classes, genders, locales and communities. The fellowship has little to do with the party leadership or the electoral battles. This is about sharing some insights we gained in various fields working in this political laboratory called the Aam Aadmi Party.
Why is AAP offering this? Is it some kind of a proselytism at play?
We were never politicians. We were, and still are common people. We are still trying to understand governance, public policy and delivering the basics to the common man. We learned a great deal about India, its people, the status quo gathering lessons from various perspectives around the world. We wish to share some of our insights to smart, highly driven and motivated individuals who would end up becoming a part of India's next-gen workforce. We wish to instil acumen on things like: methods of analysing problems, building perspectives on broad issues, or examining implications of our decisions.
What's in it for me?
You get to pick a track you want to learn about. You get to be tutored on contemporary matters across dimensions from highly progressive leaders. You get to meet party workers, understand how decision making takes place in the capital today. You get to see connections between theories, established practices from other parts of the world and their adaptation in the Indian context. We hope that you carry your newfound perspective for more empathetic action in whatever path you take in the future.
Who should apply? What is expected from me?
Empathetic students interested in specified tracks are encouraged to apply. We expect strong communication skills, ability to read/grasp concepts from various topics, participate in team case studies, discussions/debates and humility to learn. We expect your sincere participation, which entails rigorous readings demanding auto-didactic capacities. You are expected to think critically, objectively reason and be highly logical. The 4 week program is expected to be a full time endeavour and we hope you can keep yourself fully available for the duration taking care of your own stay, transport and food.
Do we get to meet AAP leaders during the program?
Indeed! Our MLAs and leaders are always busy in their duties to the public. However, our program consists of talks and discussions with a few selected ones. Some of the sessions would be led by the youth leaders who bring fresh perspective, and a deeper connection to millennials.
Can I use this to get my foot into the door to join the party?
You'd be exposed to some of the inner workings of our constantly evolving organisation. We expect you to build networks with people and develop affinity towards issues, questions and problems to work on. Your participation in the fellowship and its various phases gives us a chance to get to know you too. If your priorities align with our vision: to build a nation of equal opportunity to all, you are always welcome.
