WOMEN SAFETY

Ayush Chauhan

Women are used to being worshipped as goddesses; people pray for their blessings. This practice in our country and society is much older than one could imagine. In the Indian society, women have always been portrayed as powerful goddesses, who are the savior of all mankind.

It can be said that because women were always viewed as a deities or an idol, they are confined to their homes and prohibited from going outside. Even if women do step outside, they are always asked or rather ordered to be in the company of a man. Or maybe that's what our society has been teaching us – Man is sole protector.

The incidents of crime against women have risen sharply over the past few years. The latest National Crime Records Bureau data reflects how incidents of rapes have gone up by 12-15%, while other crimes have risen by 3-5%. Perpetrators are far from being prosecuted and the years of patriarchal conditioning continues to further the sick mindsets of the people. In many of the families, it is still believed that an incident where the woman gets harassed, molested, or raped- she brings shame to the family. No one encourages victims of sexual abuse or assault to speak up about it or even register complaints, thanks to the stigma attached to such crimes. Crime against women are on the rise and it makes one wonder- have we become so primitive in our actions, so insensitive and inhumane, that we fail to even recognize the other as mere humans?

Reporting any crime, especially in rural India is not an easy job. The stigma of crimes like sexual assault is so pervasive that the first response to a rape, molestation, stalking etc. is more often than not victim-shaming. Women are ridiculed for their choice of clothes and labelled as someone who were ‘asking for it’, when they are teased, groped or raped. Even politicians, religious Gurus or people holding public office, have gone on record to make statements that make you doubt their sanity in the first place. Westernization is another term used by the patriarchal society which is used as an excuse for harassing women for being too "modern".

Conviction rates in India are very poor. Only one in four rape cases result in a prosecution.

Women must never compromise or adjust in an unhealthy environment, and must always speak up against any inconvenience or passive eve teasing. Not raising their voices against the unjust practices of the society only gives the perpetrators an upper hand and helps them get away with their shameful actions. Active dialogue and debate must be carried out to change our thought process. Proper education and implementation of stricter laws are also required which would certainly help change mindsets.
