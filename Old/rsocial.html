Social and Political Implications of Disintegration of European Union

Anushka Sikka

"The modern world, for better or worse, springs from Europe."

Europe is the continent that gave birth to the concept of democracy, politics and citizenship. The Industrial revolution is yet another by product that has now taken forms such as globalisation, basically the promulgation of borderless economy or the white globe concept. It is an amalgamation of 28 European Countries governed by common economic, social and security policies. The EU was created by the Maastricht Treaty, came into force on November 1, 1993. The formation of European continent was characterised by numerous geographically proximate states and people. The reason for this lies in the geography of the region. Europe's major rivers like Danube do not meet. This partly explains why Europe comprises of so many countries in a relatively smaller space. For Instance The Danube Basin produces geographical advantages of terrain, the interconnection of rivers provide natural borders alongwith the benefit of navigation. As a result, encouraging greater chances of economic influence in world trade system. This has been accompanied by political-economic measures such as creation of a uniform currency (Euro). However, this has led to both creation of ties and tensions in the continent. The source for the latter was the management of Europe's intrinsic diversities. In the current scenario, headlines such as the Greece repression, Eurozone crisis, Question of Turkey's membership or the deliberation behind the Brexit have exposed EU to leakages and cracks that are widening annually. This report aims to identify the irritants behind the same. The major focus of the report shall revolve around how the reshuffling of Europe's political geography is taking place in four paradigms:The Reshuffling of Elite Countries that set the foundation stones for the Union. Secondly, how the history has been profitable to the northern europe than those in the south for several centuries. The industrialisation of north before the south has been responsible for the divide. Thirdly, the shrinking core and the enlargement of periphery countries. The countries that perceived themselves as the heart of Europe have now been relegated to insignificant status due to their low economic performance. In addition to this, there has emerged a division between creditor and debtor states creating bitter scuffles, encouraging the creditor nations to withdraw from EU. Fourthly, the shifting central command from Brussels to Germany economically has been causing grave insecurities for France in the backdrop of Franco-Prussian War. This has led to a Union without any strong central authority, incapable of holding it together. The final observation is lack of a common shared vision of European Integration. Implying, people identify with their countries and not with the idea of being an European. It must be noted that a similar phenomenon was observed before the disintegration of soviet union. The paper is a sum of divergence and convergence amongst the member states of EU and the related cleavages. Alongwith, the friction of market integration against social cohesion.
