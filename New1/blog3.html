DUSU Elections and Indian Politics

Nilesh Mudgil

Delhi University Students Union elections are the largest youth elections that take place every year, in the University of Delhi. It witnesses the participation by Youth wings of major Political parties, either at National or State Level such as National Students Union Of India by Congress, Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad by BJP, All India Students Union by Communist party and Chhatra Yuva Sangharsh Samiti by AAP. These elections have a prominent position in election system in the country as it influences the youth in their understanding the elections, which at a certain level also further influences their support and membership in later life, for example Arun Jaitley, Ramesh Bidhuri, Ajay Maken etc. started their career from student politics.

But the frequently asked question is that, does it reflect the direction of political wave in the country despite its regionalised politics and give an image of people’s preference? Or is it foolishness to compare the relatively smaller scale DUSU elections with national or State (here Delhi’s) preference? Before taking a view on this, we need to reiterate that majority of population in India and Delhi specifically, is youth, which makes it very powerful. The party that captures the youth votes, captures the Nation. But we also need to keep in mind that DUSU elections are just limited to Delhi University, where only DU students can vote.

The General Election are just round the corner. Parties and candidates’ campaigns are in full swing. The much anticipated DUSU election results would give a glimpse of the possible outcome of the General Election, as DUSU elections are reflective of the opinions of around 1.5 lakh students in the University. In 2013 and 2014 DUSU elections, ABVP candidates were elected for majority of the posts which could be credited to the ‘Modi wave’. Therefore, it appears DUSU elections get highly influenced by political environment and conditions in the country and Delhi.

At the same time, it should not be denied that Delhi’s population is about 20,000,000. And of these 1.5 lakh student voters, only 60-70% come out to vote. So, it implies towards an incapacity to judge the wave or political inclination of even Delhi, through these elections. For instance, the current president in DUSU is from NSUI, a Congress wing and it's nowhere in corroboration with neither the Delhi Assembly nor the Central government. AAP government came to power two times in Delhi Assembly with a phenomenal win, but its Youth wing couldn’t get the same support.

The impact that General elections and Assembly polls have on these elections, can’t be denied. But elections outcomes are very uncertain and difficult to predict through these small and regional elections because of the complexities that exist with people’s mind and constantly changing environment.
